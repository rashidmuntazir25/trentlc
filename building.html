Building AI

Once you know what AI is, the next question is how it actually works. Building AI walks you through the core methods behind modern systems and lets you decide how technical to get — from following the logic in plain English to writing real Python of your own.

5 chapters 3 difficulty levels Optional coding
Start the course

A little curiosity about maths and logic helps, but every concept is introduced gently. We recommend finishing Introduction to AI first.

What you will cover

The five chapters

Each chapter pairs the ideas with worked examples, so you see not just what a method does but why it works. Take them in order to get the most from the course.

  1. 1

    Getting started with AI

    Frame a real problem in a way a machine can tackle, and learn the building blocks that every later chapter relies on.

  2. 2

    Dealing with uncertainty

    Use probability to make sound decisions when the data is incomplete — the everyday reality for any system working in the real world.

  3. 3

    Machine learning

    Train models that spot patterns and make predictions, and learn how to judge honestly whether they are any good.

  4. 4

    Neural networks

    Build up from a single artificial neuron to the layered networks behind today’s most capable AI, one clear step at a time.

  5. 5

    Conclusion

    Pull the threads together, reflect on what you can now build responsibly, and plan where to take your skills next.

Choose your depth

Three difficulty levels, one course

The same chapters, pitched three ways. Pick the level that matches your confidence — or start gently and step up as the ideas click into place.

Beginner

Follow the ideas

Concept-first and completely code-free. You will reason through each method using plain language, diagrams and everyday analogies — ideal if you want the understanding without the syntax.

Intermediate

Read the logic

Step up to pseudocode and short, readable snippets of Python. You will trace how an algorithm turns into working steps, building intuition for how real systems are put together.

Advanced

Write the code

Roll up your sleeves and implement the methods yourself. You will work with full Python examples and tackle exercises that ask you to extend and adapt them.

Design your own AI project

The course finishes with something you make. You will scope a small AI project around a problem you actually care about, choose a sensible approach, and outline how you would build and evaluate it. It is the moment the theory becomes yours.
