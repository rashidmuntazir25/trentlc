Introduction to AI

A plain-language introduction to what artificial intelligence really is, what it can do today, and what it means for the rest of us. Across six chapters you will build a clear, honest mental model of the technology — and learn to tell genuine capability from hype.

6 chapters ~20 hours, self-paced Free to enrol
Start the course

No prior knowledge needed. This course assumes no programming experience and no maths beyond what you met at school. If you can read this page, you are ready.

What you will cover

The six chapters

Each chapter mixes short readings with light exercises that check your understanding. Work through them in order — every idea builds on the one before.

  1. 1

    What is AI?

    Explore how we define artificial intelligence, why that definition keeps shifting, and what really separates a smart system from clever marketing.

  2. 2

    AI problem solving

    Discover how machines navigate choices, search through huge numbers of possibilities, and plan a route towards a goal.

  3. 3

    Real-world AI

    See how probability lets AI reason sensibly even when information is incomplete, noisy or downright contradictory.

  4. 4

    Machine learning

    Understand how a computer can improve at a task by learning from examples instead of following a fixed list of instructions.

  5. 5

    Neural networks

    Take a friendly look inside the brain-inspired models that power image recognition, language tools and much of today’s AI.

  6. 6

    Implications

    Weigh the social, ethical and economic effects of putting AI to work — from jobs and fairness to privacy and accountability.

How the course works

  • Read at your own pace — start, pause and return whenever suits you
  • Short exercises after each section to check understanding
  • A free certificate of completion once you finish all six chapters
  • Open to anyone, anywhere, at no cost
